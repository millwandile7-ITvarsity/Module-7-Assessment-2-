Cheese Burger

A cheeseburger is a hamburger with cheese on top of the meat flap. The designation is a suitcase word, as well as a composition from the words "cheese" and "hamburger". The cheese is usually cut, after which it is added just before the hamburger is fully cooked, allowing it to melt. Cheeseburgers are often served with fillings such as lettuce, tomato, onions, gherkins, mustard, mayonnaise or tomato sauce. In fast - food eateries, the characteristically used cheese is processed cheese, although alternation can occur, such as Cheddar, Mozzarella, Pepper Jack, as well as Swiss and blue cheese types. The cheese inside the cheeseburger can differ significantly from nutritional value: A single slice of Cheddar can add 95 calories and 5 grams of saturated fats to a burger. [1] Other types and quantities of cheese will result in differences depending on their respective nutritional value.
